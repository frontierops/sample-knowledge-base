# Lightsaber Combat Forms

Lightsaber combat was traditionally organized into seven classical forms, each a distinct discipline
with its own philosophy, strengths, and weaknesses. A practiced duelist studied multiple forms and chose
among them according to the opponent and the situation.

The forms ranged from the foundational Form I (Shii-Cho) taught to every initiate, through the
blaster-deflecting precision of Form III (Soresu) favored by
[Obi-Wan Kenobi](../characters/obi-wan-kenobi.md), to the aggressive power of Form V and the acrobatic
Form IV (Ataru) suited to a small, agile duelist like [Yoda](../characters/yoda.md). The most dangerous
was the unorthodox Form VII (Juyo/Vaapad), which channeled a controlled fury and was mastered by
[Mace Windu](../characters/mace-windu.md). Because a [lightsaber](../technology/lightsaber.md) had no
weight to its blade, the forms emphasized control, footwork, and a constant reliance on
[the Force](the-force.md) to anticipate the opponent rather than raw strength.
