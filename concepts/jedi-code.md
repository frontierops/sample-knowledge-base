# The Jedi Code

The Jedi Code is the guiding philosophy of the [Jedi Order](../factions/jedi-order.md), a set of
principles meant to keep its members aligned with the light side of [the Force](the-force.md) and free
of the passions that lead to the dark side.

The Code is often expressed in a short mantra contrasting emotion with peace, ignorance with knowledge,
passion with serenity, and ultimately death with the Force. In practice it counseled Jedi to act with
calm and compassion, to serve rather than dominate, and above all to avoid attachment — possessive
emotional bonds that could give rise to fear and anger. This prohibition on attachment proved tragic for
[Anakin Skywalker](../characters/darth-vader.md), whose forbidden love and fear of loss made him
vulnerable to the [Sith](../factions/sith.md). Critics, including some Jedi, argued that the Order's
rigid reading of the Code left it inflexible in the Order's final years.
