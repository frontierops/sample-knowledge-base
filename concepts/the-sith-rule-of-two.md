# The Rule of Two

The Rule of Two was the central doctrine of the [Sith](../factions/sith.md) order in its final
millennium: at any time there could be only two Sith — a master, to embody the power of the dark side,
and an apprentice, to crave it and one day seize it.

The rule was established by Darth Bane after the ancient Sith nearly destroyed themselves through
infighting and unchecked ambition. By limiting their number to two, the Sith concentrated their strength,
avoided self-destructive civil wars, and — crucially — operated in secret, hidden from the
[Jedi](../factions/jedi-order.md) for a thousand years. The apprentice was expected to eventually
overthrow the master, ensuring that only the strongest survived. The doctrine reached its culmination in
[Darth Sidious](../characters/palpatine.md), who, with his apprentice
[Darth Vader](../characters/darth-vader.md), finally achieved the Sith's long-sought revenge against the
Jedi and seized control of the galaxy.
